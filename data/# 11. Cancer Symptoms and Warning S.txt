# 11. Cancer Symptoms and Warning Signs

## Overview

Cancer can cause a wide range of symptoms depending on its type, location, size, and stage. In many cases, early-stage cancer may not cause noticeable symptoms. As cancer grows and spreads, symptoms become more apparent.

Recognizing potential warning signs can lead to earlier diagnosis and treatment, which often improves outcomes. However, many cancer symptoms can also be caused by non-cancerous conditions, so medical evaluation is important.

---

# What Are Cancer Symptoms?

Cancer symptoms are physical or functional changes caused by:

* Growth of a tumor
* Invasion of nearby tissues
* Hormonal changes
* Immune system responses
* Spread of cancer to other organs

Symptoms vary widely among individuals and cancer types.

---

# General Warning Signs of Cancer

Certain symptoms may occur in many different types of cancer.

---

## 1. Unexplained Weight Loss

### Description

Losing weight without changes in diet or exercise.

### Why It Happens

Cancer cells consume large amounts of energy and may alter metabolism.

### Common Associated Cancers

* Pancreatic cancer
* Lung cancer
* Stomach cancer
* Esophageal cancer

### Warning Sign

Unintentional loss of 10 pounds (4.5 kg) or more should be evaluated.

---

## 2. Persistent Fatigue

### Description

Extreme tiredness that does not improve with rest.

### Causes

* Anemia
* Increased energy demands
* Inflammation
* Treatment effects

### Common Associated Cancers

* Leukemia
* Lymphoma
* Colon cancer

---

## 3. Fever

### Description

Repeated or persistent fever without infection.

### Causes

Cancer may trigger immune responses that elevate body temperature.

### Common Associated Cancers

* Leukemia
* Lymphoma
* Kidney cancer

---

## 4. Pain

### Description

Persistent or unexplained pain.

### Examples

* Bone pain
* Headaches
* Abdominal pain
* Back pain

### Common Associated Cancers

* Bone cancer
* Brain tumors
* Pancreatic cancer

---

# Changes in the Skin

The skin may provide important clues about underlying cancer.

---

## Yellowing of Skin (Jaundice)

May indicate:

* Liver cancer
* Pancreatic cancer

---

## Darkening of Skin

Can occur with certain cancers or hormonal changes.

---

## Persistent Itching

Sometimes associated with:

* Lymphoma
* Liver disease

---

## New or Changing Moles

Important warning sign of melanoma.

### ABCDE Rule

**A – Asymmetry**
One half differs from the other.

**B – Border**
Irregular edges.

**C – Color**
Uneven colors.

**D – Diameter**
Greater than 6 mm.

**E – Evolving**
Changing size, shape, or color.

---

# Lumps and Swellings

## New Lump or Mass

A painless lump can be an early sign of cancer.

### Common Locations

* Breast
* Neck
* Testicle
* Armpit

### Associated Cancers

* Breast cancer
* Thyroid cancer
* Lymphoma

---

## Enlarged Lymph Nodes

May indicate:

* Lymphoma
* Leukemia
* Metastatic cancer

Common sites:

* Neck
* Armpits
* Groin

---

# Changes in Bowel Habits

Persistent bowel changes should be evaluated.

---

## Constipation

Can be associated with:

* Colorectal cancer
* Ovarian cancer

---

## Diarrhea

Persistent diarrhea may indicate gastrointestinal cancers.

---

## Narrow Stools

May suggest a blockage caused by colorectal cancer.

---

## Blood in Stool

May appear:

* Bright red
* Dark red
* Black

Associated cancers:

* Colorectal cancer
* Anal cancer

---

# Changes in Urination

## Blood in Urine (Hematuria)

Possible causes:

* Bladder cancer
* Kidney cancer

---

## Frequent Urination

May occur with:

* Prostate cancer
* Bladder cancer

---

## Painful Urination

Requires medical evaluation.

---

# Persistent Cough or Hoarseness

## Chronic Cough

A cough lasting several weeks may indicate:

* Lung cancer
* Throat cancer

---

## Coughing Up Blood

A serious warning sign requiring immediate medical attention.

---

## Hoarseness

Persistent voice changes may indicate:

* Laryngeal cancer
* Thyroid cancer

---

# Difficulty Swallowing

## Dysphagia

Difficulty swallowing food or liquids.

### Associated Cancers

* Esophageal cancer
* Throat cancer
* Stomach cancer

---

# Unusual Bleeding or Discharge

Abnormal bleeding can be an important warning sign.

---

## Vaginal Bleeding

Especially concerning:

* After menopause
* Between periods
* After intercourse

Associated cancers:

* Cervical cancer
* Endometrial cancer

---

## Nipple Discharge

Especially if bloody.

Associated with:

* Breast cancer

---

## Rectal Bleeding

May indicate:

* Colorectal cancer
* Anal cancer

---

# Mouth Changes

## Persistent Mouth Sores

Sores that do not heal within two weeks require evaluation.

---

## White or Red Patches

Possible signs of oral cancer.

---

## Difficulty Moving the Jaw

Can occur with advanced oral cancers.

---

# Neurological Symptoms

Cancer affecting the brain or nervous system may cause:

---

## Persistent Headaches

Especially if worsening over time.

---

## Seizures

Can occur with brain tumors.

---

## Weakness or Numbness

May indicate nervous system involvement.

---

## Vision Changes

Examples:

* Blurred vision
* Double vision

---

# Respiratory Symptoms

## Shortness of Breath

Possible causes:

* Lung cancer
* Pleural tumors

---

## Chest Pain

Persistent pain should be investigated.

---

## Wheezing

May result from airway obstruction.

---

# Bone and Joint Symptoms

## Bone Pain

Common in:

* Bone cancer
* Multiple myeloma
* Metastatic cancer

---

## Fractures

Bones weakened by cancer may fracture more easily.

---

# Symptoms of Advanced Cancer

When cancer spreads, symptoms may include:

### Severe Fatigue

Persistent exhaustion.

---

### Significant Weight Loss

Often referred to as cancer cachexia.

---

### Chronic Pain

May involve bones or internal organs.

---

### Organ Dysfunction

Depending on the location of metastasis.

---

# When to Seek Medical Attention

A healthcare professional should be consulted if any of the following persist:

* Unexplained weight loss
* Persistent fatigue
* New lumps
* Blood in stool or urine
* Persistent cough
* Difficulty swallowing
* Abnormal bleeding
* Changes in moles
* Ongoing pain

Persistence for more than a few weeks generally warrants evaluation.

---

# Importance of Early Detection

Many cancers are highly treatable when diagnosed early.

Benefits of early detection:

* Less aggressive treatment
* Higher survival rates
* Better quality of life
* Reduced complications

Screening programs play an important role in detecting cancer before symptoms develop.

---

# Key Takeaways

* Cancer symptoms vary depending on the type and stage of disease.
* Early cancer may cause no symptoms.
* Common warning signs include unexplained weight loss, fatigue, pain, persistent cough, abnormal bleeding, and new lumps.
* Changes in skin, bowel habits, urination, and swallowing should not be ignored.
* Many symptoms can be caused by non-cancerous conditions, but persistent symptoms require medical evaluation.
* Early detection significantly improves treatment outcomes and survival.
* Awareness of warning signs helps individuals seek timely medical care.
