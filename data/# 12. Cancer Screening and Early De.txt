# 12. Cancer Screening and Early Detection

## Overview

Cancer screening and early detection are among the most effective strategies for reducing cancer-related deaths. Screening aims to identify cancer or precancerous conditions before symptoms appear, allowing treatment to begin at an earlier and often more curable stage.

Many cancers can be successfully treated when detected early. In some cases, screening can even prevent cancer by identifying and removing precancerous lesions before they become malignant.

---

# What is Cancer Screening?

Cancer screening is the process of testing individuals who do not have symptoms to look for signs of cancer or precancerous changes.

### Goals of Screening

* Detect cancer early
* Improve survival rates
* Reduce cancer-related deaths
* Prevent cancer development in some cases
* Reduce treatment complexity

---

# Difference Between Screening and Diagnosis

| Screening                                | Diagnosis                           |
| ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Performed before symptoms appear         | Performed after symptoms develop    |
| Identifies possible abnormalities        | Confirms whether cancer is present  |
| Used in healthy or high-risk individuals | Used in symptomatic individuals     |
| May require follow-up tests              | Usually involves definitive testing |

---

# Why Early Detection Matters

Cancer often progresses through stages.

### Early-Stage Cancer

Characteristics:

* Smaller tumors
* Limited spread
* Easier treatment
* Higher cure rates

### Advanced Cancer

Characteristics:

* Larger tumors
* Metastasis
* More complex treatment
* Lower survival rates

---

# Types of Cancer Screening

Screening methods vary depending on the cancer type.

Common approaches include:

* Imaging tests
* Laboratory tests
* Endoscopic procedures
* Genetic testing
* Physical examinations

---

# Breast Cancer Screening

## Mammography

### What is Mammography?

A low-dose X-ray of the breast used to detect abnormalities.

### Benefits

* Detects cancer before symptoms appear
* Reduces mortality
* Identifies small tumors

### Findings

May detect:

* Masses
* Calcifications
* Structural changes

---

## Breast MRI

Used for:

* High-risk individuals
* Dense breast tissue
* Additional evaluation

---

## Clinical Breast Examination

Physical examination by a healthcare provider.

---

# Cervical Cancer Screening

## Pap Test (Pap Smear)

### Purpose

Detects abnormal cervical cells before cancer develops.

### Procedure

Cells are collected from the cervix and examined under a microscope.

### Benefits

Can identify precancerous lesions years before cancer develops.

---

## HPV Testing

### Purpose

Detects high-risk strains of Human Papillomavirus (HPV).

Important high-risk types:

* HPV-16
* HPV-18

---

## Co-Testing

Combines:

* Pap test
* HPV testing

Provides improved screening accuracy.

---

# Colorectal Cancer Screening

## Colonoscopy

### Overview

The gold-standard screening test.

### Advantages

* Direct visualization of the colon
* Removal of polyps
* Tissue biopsy if necessary

### Importance

Can prevent cancer by removing precancerous polyps.

---

## Fecal Immunochemical Test (FIT)

### Purpose

Detects hidden blood in stool.

### Advantages

* Non-invasive
* Convenient

---

## Stool DNA Testing

Detects abnormal DNA associated with colorectal cancer.

---

## CT Colonography

Virtual colonoscopy using CT imaging.

---

# Lung Cancer Screening

## Low-Dose Computed Tomography (LDCT)

### Purpose

Detects lung cancer in high-risk individuals.

### Recommended For

People with:

* Significant smoking history
* Older age
* Elevated lung cancer risk

### Benefits

Detects cancers at earlier stages.

---

# Prostate Cancer Screening

## Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) Test

### What is PSA?

A protein produced by the prostate gland.

### Screening Method

Blood test measuring PSA levels.

### Limitations

Elevated PSA may occur due to:

* Prostate cancer
* Benign enlargement
* Infection

---

## Digital Rectal Examination (DRE)

Physical examination of the prostate.

Used alongside PSA testing.

---

# Skin Cancer Screening

## Skin Self-Examination

Individuals inspect their skin regularly.

### Areas to Check

* Face
* Neck
* Arms
* Legs
* Back
* Scalp

---

## Dermatologic Examination

Professional skin evaluation.

---

## ABCDE Rule for Melanoma

### A – Asymmetry

Uneven shape.

### B – Border

Irregular edges.

### C – Color

Uneven coloration.

### D – Diameter

Greater than 6 mm.

### E – Evolving

Changing appearance.

---

# Oral Cancer Screening

## Visual Oral Examination

Healthcare providers inspect:

* Lips
* Tongue
* Gums
* Throat

### Warning Signs

* White patches
* Red patches
* Non-healing ulcers

---

# Liver Cancer Surveillance

High-risk individuals may undergo regular monitoring.

## Ultrasound

Detects liver abnormalities.

---

## Alpha-Fetoprotein (AFP)

Blood test used in surveillance programs.

Commonly used in patients with:

* Chronic hepatitis B
* Chronic hepatitis C
* Cirrhosis

---

# Genetic Testing and Cancer Risk Assessment

Some individuals have inherited cancer risk.

## Genetic Testing

Identifies inherited mutations.

Examples:

### BRCA1 and BRCA2

Associated with:

* Breast cancer
* Ovarian cancer
* Prostate cancer

---

### Lynch Syndrome Testing

Associated with:

* Colorectal cancer
* Endometrial cancer

---

## Benefits

* Identifies high-risk individuals
* Guides screening strategies
* Supports preventive interventions

---

# Imaging-Based Screening

## X-Ray

Used in specific situations but limited as a screening tool.

---

## Ultrasound

Useful for:

* Breast evaluation
* Liver surveillance
* Thyroid assessment

---

## CT Scan

Provides detailed cross-sectional images.

---

## MRI

Offers excellent soft tissue visualization.

---

# Tumor Markers and Blood Tests

Tumor markers are substances that may be elevated in cancer.

Examples:

| Marker | Associated Cancer |
| ------ | ----------------- |
| PSA    | Prostate Cancer   |
| AFP    | Liver Cancer      |
| CEA    | Colorectal Cancer |
| CA-125 | Ovarian Cancer    |

### Important Note

Tumor markers alone cannot diagnose cancer.

---

# Benefits of Cancer Screening

## Earlier Diagnosis

Detects cancer before symptoms appear.

---

## Better Treatment Outcomes

Early cancers are generally easier to treat.

---

## Reduced Mortality

Screening programs lower cancer death rates.

---

## Prevention

Some tests remove precancerous lesions.

Example:

* Colonoscopy removing polyps.

---

# Limitations of Screening

## False Positives

Abnormal results despite absence of cancer.

May cause:

* Anxiety
* Additional testing

---

## False Negatives

Cancer may be present despite normal screening results.

---

## Overdiagnosis

Detection of cancers that may never become harmful.

---

# High-Risk Individuals

Certain people require closer monitoring.

### Examples

* Strong family history
* Genetic mutations
* Previous cancer diagnosis
* Chronic viral infections
* Heavy smokers

---

# Recommended Cancer Prevention Measures

Screening should be combined with:

### Healthy Lifestyle

* Balanced diet
* Regular exercise
* Healthy weight

---

### Tobacco Avoidance

Major cancer prevention strategy.

---

### Limiting Alcohol

Reduces cancer risk.

---

### Vaccination

Important vaccines include:

* Gardasil 9
* Cervarix
* Engerix-B

---

# Future Directions in Early Detection

Emerging technologies include:

## Liquid Biopsy

Detects cancer-related DNA in blood.

### Potential Benefits

* Earlier diagnosis
* Minimal invasiveness
* Monitoring treatment response

---

## Artificial Intelligence (AI)

AI is increasingly used for:

* Mammogram interpretation
* Pathology analysis
* Risk prediction

---

## Multi-Cancer Early Detection Tests

Blood tests designed to detect multiple cancer types simultaneously.

---

# Key Takeaways

* Cancer screening identifies cancer or precancerous conditions before symptoms develop.
* Early detection greatly improves treatment success and survival rates.
* Major screening tools include mammography, Pap testing, HPV testing, colonoscopy, PSA testing, skin examinations, and low-dose CT scans.
* Genetic testing helps identify individuals at increased cancer risk.
* Screening has benefits and limitations, including false positives and false negatives.
* High-risk individuals often require enhanced surveillance.
* Combining screening with healthy lifestyle choices provides the greatest protection against cancer.
* Emerging technologies such as liquid biopsy and AI may transform future cancer detection.
