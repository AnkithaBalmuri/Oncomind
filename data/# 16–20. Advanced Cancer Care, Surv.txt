# 16. Cancer Prevention

## What is Cancer Prevention?

Cancer prevention refers to actions that reduce the risk of developing cancer.

According to global health organizations, a significant proportion of cancers can be prevented through lifestyle modifications, vaccination, and screening programs.

---

## Primary Prevention

Primary prevention aims to stop cancer before it develops.

### Avoid Tobacco

Tobacco is the leading preventable cause of cancer.

Associated cancers:

* Lung cancer
* Oral cancer
* Bladder cancer
* Pancreatic cancer
* Kidney cancer

### Recommendations

* Do not smoke.
* Avoid secondhand smoke.
* Quit tobacco products.

---

## Healthy Diet

A balanced diet may lower cancer risk.

### Recommended Foods

* Fruits
* Vegetables
* Whole grains
* Legumes
* Nuts

### Limit

* Processed meat
* Excess red meat
* Sugary beverages
* Highly processed foods

---

## Physical Activity

Regular exercise helps:

* Maintain healthy weight
* Improve immune function
* Reduce inflammation

### Recommended

At least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week.

---

## Weight Management

Obesity increases risk for:

* Breast cancer
* Colorectal cancer
* Endometrial cancer
* Liver cancer
* Kidney cancer

Maintaining a healthy body weight reduces risk.

---

## Alcohol Reduction

Alcohol consumption is linked to:

* Breast cancer
* Liver cancer
* Colorectal cancer
* Oral cancer

Reducing alcohol intake lowers risk.

---

## UV Protection

Prevents skin cancer.

### Methods

* Sunscreen
* Protective clothing
* Avoid tanning beds

---

## Vaccination

Certain cancers can be prevented through vaccination.

### HPV Vaccination

Examples:

* Gardasil 9
* Cervarix

Prevents many HPV-related cancers.

---

### Hepatitis B Vaccination

Example:

* Engerix-B

Helps prevent liver cancer.

---

## Secondary Prevention

Secondary prevention focuses on early detection.

Examples:

* Mammography
* Colonoscopy
* Pap testing
* HPV testing
* PSA testing

---

# 17. Cancer Survivorship and Follow-Up Care

## What is Cancer Survivorship?

Cancer survivorship begins at diagnosis and continues throughout life.

A cancer survivor is any person who has been diagnosed with cancer, regardless of treatment status.

---

## Goals of Survivorship Care

* Detect recurrence
* Manage side effects
* Improve quality of life
* Support emotional well-being
* Monitor long-term health

---

## Follow-Up Care

Patients require regular medical evaluations after treatment.

### Components

* Physical examinations
* Blood tests
* Imaging studies
* Symptom monitoring

---

## Monitoring for Recurrence

Recurrence can occur:

### Local Recurrence

Cancer returns at original site.

### Regional Recurrence

Cancer returns in nearby lymph nodes.

### Distant Recurrence

Cancer returns in distant organs.

---

## Long-Term Treatment Effects

Cancer treatment may cause:

### Physical Effects

* Fatigue
* Pain
* Neuropathy
* Heart disease
* Fertility problems

---

### Psychological Effects

* Anxiety
* Depression
* Fear of recurrence

---

## Survivorship Support

### Nutritional Counseling

Promotes healthy recovery.

---

### Rehabilitation

Improves mobility and function.

---

### Mental Health Support

Addresses emotional challenges.

---

### Support Groups

Provide peer support and education.

---

# 18. Cancer Complications and Oncologic Emergencies

## Overview

Cancer and its treatments can cause serious complications requiring immediate medical attention.

---

# Common Cancer Complications

## Infection

Cancer patients often have weakened immune systems.

### Causes

* Chemotherapy
* Bone marrow suppression
* Blood cancers

### Symptoms

* Fever
* Chills
* Weakness

---

## Anemia

Low red blood cell count.

### Symptoms

* Fatigue
* Dizziness
* Shortness of breath

---

## Thrombocytopenia

Low platelet count.

### Symptoms

* Easy bruising
* Bleeding
* Nosebleeds

---

## Malnutrition

Caused by:

* Reduced appetite
* Treatment side effects
* Increased metabolic demands

---

## Cancer Pain

May result from:

* Tumor growth
* Bone metastases
* Nerve compression

---

# Oncologic Emergencies

## Febrile Neutropenia

A medical emergency characterized by:

* Fever
* Extremely low white blood cell count

Common after chemotherapy.

---

## Spinal Cord Compression

Occurs when tumors compress the spinal cord.

### Symptoms

* Back pain
* Weakness
* Paralysis

Requires urgent treatment.

---

## Superior Vena Cava Syndrome

Compression of the superior vena cava.

### Symptoms

* Facial swelling
* Neck vein enlargement
* Breathing difficulties

---

## Hypercalcemia of Malignancy

High calcium levels caused by cancer.

### Symptoms

* Confusion
* Dehydration
* Kidney problems

---

## Tumor Lysis Syndrome

Rapid destruction of cancer cells.

Common in:

* Leukemia
* Lymphoma

Can cause life-threatening electrolyte abnormalities.

---

---

# Types of Cancer Research

## Basic Research

Studies:

* Cancer biology
* Genetics
* Molecular pathways

---

## Translational Research

Converts laboratory discoveries into clinical applications.

---

## Clinical Research

Tests new interventions in patients.

---

# Clinical Trials

## Definition

Research studies that evaluate new medical approaches.

---

## Purpose

* Assess safety
* Determine effectiveness
* Improve patient outcomes

---

# Clinical Trial Phases

## Phase I

Evaluates safety.

### Goal

Determine safe dosage.

---

## Phase II

Evaluates effectiveness.

### Goal

Assess treatment activity.

---

## Phase III

Compares new treatment against standard therapy.

### Goal

Determine superiority or equivalence.

---

## Phase IV

Conducted after approval.

### Goal

Monitor long-term safety.

---

# Benefits of Clinical Trials

Participants may gain:

* Access to new therapies
* Advanced monitoring
* Contribution to medical progress

---

# Challenges

* Eligibility restrictions
* Potential side effects
* Uncertain outcomes

---

# Major Research Areas

---

## Targeted Therapy

Targets specific molecular abnormalities.

---

## Precision Medicine

Individualized treatment based on tumor genetics.

---

## Liquid Biopsy

Detects tumor DNA in blood.

---

## Cancer Vaccines

* Molecular profiling
* Biomarker testing
* Personalized therapies

AI is transforming cancer care.

### Applications

#### Early Detection

Analyzing imaging studies.

---

#### Pathology

Improving cancer classification.

---

#### Treatment Planning

Predicting treatment response.

---

#### Drug Discovery

Accelerating development of new therapies.

* Detect cancer early
* Monitor recurrence

* Immune cell modification

Expanding use beyond blood cancers.

---

## Personalized Cancer Vaccines

## Combination Immunotherapy
