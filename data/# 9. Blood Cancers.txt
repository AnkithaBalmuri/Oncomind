# 9. Blood Cancers

## Overview

Blood cancers, also known as **hematologic cancers**, are cancers that affect the blood, bone marrow, lymphatic system, and immune system. Unlike many other cancers, blood cancers usually do not form solid tumors. Instead, they affect the production and function of blood cells.

Blood cancers interfere with the body's ability to:

* Fight infections
* Carry oxygen
* Control bleeding
* Maintain a healthy immune system

The three major categories of blood cancer are:

1. Leukemia
2. Lymphoma
3. Multiple Myeloma

---

# What is Blood?

Blood consists of several important components.

## Red Blood Cells (RBCs)

Function:

* Carry oxygen from the lungs to body tissues.
* Remove carbon dioxide.

---

## White Blood Cells (WBCs)

Function:

* Protect the body from infections.
* Support immune responses.

---

## Platelets

Function:

* Help blood clot.
* Prevent excessive bleeding.

---

## Plasma

Function:

* Liquid portion of blood.
* Transports nutrients, hormones, and waste products.

---

# What are Blood Cancers?

Blood cancers occur when abnormal blood cells grow uncontrollably and interfere with normal blood cell production.

### Effects of Blood Cancer

* Reduced healthy blood cells
* Increased infections
* Anemia
* Bleeding problems
* Organ damage

Most blood cancers begin in the bone marrow or lymphatic system.

---

# Bone Marrow and Lymphatic System

## Bone Marrow

Soft tissue inside bones responsible for producing blood cells.

Produces:

* Red blood cells
* White blood cells
* Platelets

---

## Lymphatic System

Part of the immune system.

Includes:

* Lymph nodes
* Spleen
* Thymus
* Lymphatic vessels

Many blood cancers originate here.

---

# Major Types of Blood Cancer

---

# 1. Leukemia

## Definition

Leukemia is a cancer of blood-forming tissues, particularly the bone marrow.

It results in the production of large numbers of abnormal white blood cells.

These abnormal cells:

* Do not function properly.
* Crowd out healthy blood cells.

---

## Types of Leukemia

### Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)

Characteristics:

* Rapidly progressing
* Common in children

Symptoms:

* Fever
* Fatigue
* Easy bruising
* Frequent infections

---

### Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML)

Characteristics:

* Fast-growing
* Common in adults

Symptoms:

* Weakness
* Bleeding
* Bone pain

---

### Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL)

Characteristics:

* Slow-growing
* Most common adult leukemia

Symptoms:

* Enlarged lymph nodes
* Fatigue
* Recurrent infections

---

### Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML)

Characteristics:

* Associated with the Philadelphia chromosome
* Slow progression initially

Symptoms:

* Weight loss
* Fatigue
* Enlarged spleen

---

# Risk Factors for Leukemia

* Radiation exposure
* Benzene exposure
* Smoking
* Previous chemotherapy
* Genetic disorders

Examples:

* Down Syndrome

---

# Diagnosis of Leukemia

### Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Evaluates blood cell abnormalities.

---

### Bone Marrow Biopsy

Essential diagnostic procedure.

---

### Genetic Testing

Identifies chromosomal abnormalities.

---

# Treatment of Leukemia

### Chemotherapy

Primary treatment for many leukemias.

---

### Targeted Therapy

Examples:

* Imatinib
* Dasatinib

---

### Stem Cell Transplantation

Replacement of diseased bone marrow.

---

### Immunotherapy

Enhances immune response against cancer cells.

---

# 2. Lymphoma

## Definition

Lymphoma is cancer of the lymphatic system.

It develops from lymphocytes, a type of white blood cell.

---

## Types of Lymphoma

### Hodgkin Lymphoma

Characteristics:

* Presence of Reed-Sternberg cells
* Often affects young adults

---

### Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma (NHL)

Characteristics:

* Large group of lymphatic cancers
* More common than Hodgkin lymphoma

---

# Symptoms of Lymphoma

### Enlarged Lymph Nodes

Common locations:

* Neck
* Armpits
* Groin

---

### Fever

Persistent or recurrent.

---

### Night Sweats

Excessive sweating during sleep.

---

### Unexplained Weight Loss

Often referred to as a "B symptom."

---

### Fatigue

Persistent tiredness.

---

# Risk Factors for Lymphoma

* Weakened immune system
* Autoimmune diseases
* Viral infections

Examples:

* Human Immunodeficiency Virus Infection
* Epstein-Barr Virus Infection

---

# Diagnosis of Lymphoma

### Lymph Node Biopsy

Most important diagnostic test.

---

### CT Scan

Evaluates disease spread.

---

### PET Scan

Detects active lymphoma sites.

---

### Bone Marrow Examination

Determines marrow involvement.

---

# Treatment of Lymphoma

### Chemotherapy

Common first-line treatment.

---

### Radiation Therapy

Used in localized disease.

---

### Immunotherapy

Examples:

* Rituximab
* Pembrolizumab

---

### Stem Cell Transplantation

Used in selected patients.

---

# 3. Multiple Myeloma

## Definition

Multiple myeloma is a cancer of plasma cells.

Plasma cells normally produce antibodies that help fight infections.

---

## How Multiple Myeloma Develops

Abnormal plasma cells:

* Multiply excessively
* Produce abnormal proteins
* Damage bones and kidneys

---

## Commonly Affected Areas

* Spine
* Skull
* Pelvis
* Ribs

---

# Symptoms of Multiple Myeloma

### Bone Pain

Especially in the back and ribs.

---

### Frequent Infections

Due to weakened immunity.

---

### Fatigue

Often caused by anemia.

---

### Kidney Problems

Abnormal proteins may damage kidneys.

---

### High Calcium Levels

Resulting from bone destruction.

---

# Risk Factors for Multiple Myeloma

* Increasing age
* Family history
* Obesity
* Exposure to certain chemicals

---

# Diagnosis of Multiple Myeloma

### Blood Tests

Detect abnormal proteins.

---

### Urine Tests

Identify myeloma proteins.

---

### Bone Marrow Biopsy

Confirms diagnosis.

---

### Imaging Studies

Examples:

* X-rays
* MRI
* CT scans
* PET scans

---

# Treatment of Multiple Myeloma

### Targeted Therapy

Examples:

* Bortezomib
* Carfilzomib

---

### Immunomodulatory Drugs

Examples:

* Lenalidomide
* Pomalidomide

---

### Chemotherapy

Used in selected cases.

---

### Stem Cell Transplantation

Standard treatment for eligible patients.

---

# Staging of Blood Cancers

## Leukemia

Often classified by:

* Acute or chronic
* Cell type involved

---

## Lymphoma

Usually staged from:

* Stage I
* Stage II
* Stage III
* Stage IV

---

## Multiple Myeloma

Commonly staged using:

* International Staging System (ISS)

---

# Complications of Blood Cancers

### Severe Infections

Due to impaired immunity.

---

### Anemia

Reduced oxygen-carrying capacity.

---

### Bleeding Disorders

Due to low platelet counts.

---

### Organ Damage

May affect:

* Liver
* Kidneys
* Spleen

---

# Prevention

Most blood cancers cannot be completely prevented.

Risk reduction strategies include:

* Avoid smoking
* Limit exposure to toxic chemicals
* Minimize unnecessary radiation exposure
* Maintain healthy body weight
* Regular medical checkups

---

# Prognosis

Prognosis depends on:

* Cancer type
* Stage
* Genetic abnormalities
* Patient age
* Response to treatment

Modern therapies have greatly improved survival rates for many blood cancers.

---

# Living with Blood Cancer

Supportive care may include:

### Infection Prevention

Vaccinations and hygiene measures.

---

### Nutritional Support

Balanced diet during treatment.

---

### Psychological Support

Managing emotional and mental health.

---

### Long-Term Follow-Up

Monitoring for recurrence and treatment effects.

---

# Key Takeaways

* Blood cancers affect blood cells, bone marrow, and the lymphatic system.
* The three major types are **Leukemia, Lymphoma, and Multiple Myeloma**.
* Symptoms often include fatigue, infections, weight loss, fever, and abnormal bleeding.
* Diagnosis commonly involves blood tests, imaging studies, and bone marrow or lymph node biopsies.
* Treatments include chemotherapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy, radiation therapy, and stem cell transplantation.
* Advances in treatment have significantly improved survival and quality of life.
* Early diagnosis and specialized care are critical for optimal outcomes.
