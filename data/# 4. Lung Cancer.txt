# 4. Lung Cancer

## Overview

Lung cancer is a disease in which abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in the lungs. It is one of the most commonly diagnosed cancers worldwide and remains one of the leading causes of cancer-related deaths. The disease often develops slowly over many years and may not cause symptoms in its early stages.

The lungs are responsible for exchanging oxygen and carbon dioxide, making them essential organs for survival. When cancer develops in the lungs, it can interfere with breathing and spread to other parts of the body.

---

# What is Lung Cancer?

Lung cancer occurs when genetic mutations cause lung cells to grow and divide uncontrollably. Instead of dying naturally, abnormal cells continue multiplying and form tumors.

Over time, these tumors can:

* Block airways
* Damage lung tissue
* Reduce oxygen exchange
* Spread to other organs

The spread of cancer from the lungs to distant organs is called **metastasis**.

---

# Anatomy of the Lungs

The respiratory system includes:

### Trachea (Windpipe)

Carries air into the lungs.

### Bronchi

Large airways branching from the trachea.

### Bronchioles

Smaller airway branches.

### Alveoli

Tiny air sacs where oxygen enters the blood.

### Pleura

Protective membrane surrounding the lungs.

Lung cancer usually begins in the cells lining the airways.

---

# Types of Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is broadly classified into two main categories.

## 1. Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)

### Overview

NSCLC accounts for approximately 85% of lung cancer cases.

### Characteristics

* Grows relatively slowly.
* More treatment options available.
* Better prognosis when detected early.

### Subtypes

#### Adenocarcinoma

Most common lung cancer type.

Characteristics:

* Begins in mucus-producing cells.
* Common in both smokers and non-smokers.
* Usually develops in outer lung regions.

---

#### Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Characteristics:

* Begins in squamous cells lining airways.
* Strongly associated with smoking.
* Usually develops near central airways.

---

#### Large Cell Carcinoma

Characteristics:

* Can occur anywhere in the lung.
* Tends to grow rapidly.
* Often diagnosed at advanced stages.

---

## 2. Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC)

### Overview

Accounts for approximately 10–15% of lung cancers.

### Characteristics

* Highly aggressive.
* Grows rapidly.
* Spreads early.
* Strongly linked to smoking.

### Stages

#### Limited Stage

Cancer remains within one side of the chest.

#### Extensive Stage

Cancer has spread beyond the chest.

---

# Causes and Risk Factors

## Smoking

The leading cause of lung cancer.

Tobacco smoke contains thousands of chemicals, many of which are carcinogens.

Risk increases with:

* Number of cigarettes smoked
* Years of smoking
* Exposure to secondhand smoke

---

## Secondhand Smoke

Non-smokers exposed to tobacco smoke also have increased risk.

---

## Radon Gas

A naturally occurring radioactive gas that can accumulate in buildings.

Long-term exposure increases lung cancer risk.

---

## Air Pollution

Exposure to polluted air can contribute to lung cancer development.

---

## Occupational Exposures

Examples include:

* Asbestos
* Arsenic
* Chromium
* Nickel
* Diesel exhaust

---

## Family History

Individuals with close relatives diagnosed with lung cancer may have elevated risk.

---

## Previous Radiation Therapy

Radiation exposure to the chest can increase future risk.

---

# Signs and Symptoms

Early lung cancer may not cause symptoms.

As the disease progresses, symptoms may include:

### Persistent Cough

A cough that does not go away or worsens over time.

---

### Coughing Up Blood

Even small amounts require medical evaluation.

---

### Chest Pain

Pain may worsen with:

* Breathing
* Coughing
* Laughing

---

### Shortness of Breath

Due to airway blockage or reduced lung function.

---

### Wheezing

Narrowed airways can produce wheezing sounds.

---

### Hoarseness

Changes in voice quality.

---

### Frequent Lung Infections

Examples:

* Pneumonia
* Bronchitis

---

### Unexplained Weight Loss

Common in advanced cancers.

---

### Fatigue

Persistent tiredness not relieved by rest.

---

# How Lung Cancer Develops

The process typically involves:

1. Exposure to carcinogens.
2. DNA damage in lung cells.
3. Accumulation of mutations.
4. Formation of abnormal cells.
5. Tumor growth.
6. Invasion of nearby tissues.
7. Metastasis.

---

# Common Sites of Metastasis

Lung cancer often spreads to:

### Brain

May cause headaches and seizures.

### Bones

May cause pain and fractures.

### Liver

May cause abdominal discomfort and jaundice.

### Adrenal Glands

Often affected in advanced disease.

---

# Screening and Early Detection

## Low-Dose CT Scan (LDCT)

The most effective screening method for high-risk individuals.

### Benefits

* Detects cancer before symptoms appear.
* Improves survival through early diagnosis.

### Recommended for High-Risk Individuals

Typically includes:

* Older adults
* Current or former heavy smokers

---

# Diagnosis

## Medical History and Physical Examination

Assessment of symptoms and risk factors.

---

## Imaging Tests

### Chest X-ray

May reveal suspicious abnormalities.

---

### CT Scan

Provides detailed images of the lungs.

---

### PET Scan

Helps identify active cancer and metastasis.

---

### MRI

Useful for detecting spread to the brain.

---

## Sputum Cytology

Microscopic examination of mucus from the lungs.

---

## Bronchoscopy

A flexible tube is inserted into the airways for examination and tissue sampling.

---

## Biopsy

Definitive diagnostic method.

Types include:

* Needle biopsy
* Bronchoscopic biopsy
* Surgical biopsy

---

# Staging of Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

## Stage I

Cancer confined to the lung.

---

## Stage II

Larger tumor and/or nearby lymph node involvement.

---

## Stage III

Spread to regional lymph nodes and nearby structures.

---

## Stage IV

Cancer has spread to distant organs.

---

# Treatment Options

Treatment depends on:

* Cancer type
* Stage
* Genetic mutations
* Patient health

---

## Surgery

Most effective for early-stage NSCLC.

### Types

#### Wedge Resection

Removal of a small lung section.

#### Lobectomy

Removal of an entire lung lobe.

#### Pneumonectomy

Removal of an entire lung.

---

## Radiation Therapy

Uses high-energy radiation to destroy cancer cells.

Common uses:

* Curative treatment
* Symptom relief

---

## Chemotherapy

Uses medications to kill rapidly dividing cells.

May be given:

* Before surgery
* After surgery
* For advanced disease

---

## Targeted Therapy

Targets specific genetic mutations.

Examples include mutations involving:

* EGFR
* ALK
* ROS1
* BRAF

Targeted therapies often have fewer side effects than chemotherapy.

---

## Immunotherapy

Helps the immune system recognize and attack cancer cells.

Commonly used in advanced NSCLC.

Examples include:

* Pembrolizumab
* Nivolumab
* Atezolizumab

---

# Side Effects of Treatment

## Surgery

* Pain
* Infection risk
* Reduced lung function

---

## Chemotherapy

* Hair loss
* Nausea
* Fatigue
* Increased infection risk

---

## Radiation Therapy

* Skin irritation
* Fatigue
* Difficulty swallowing

---

## Immunotherapy

* Inflammation of organs
* Skin reactions
* Immune-related side effects

---

# Prevention

## Avoid Smoking

The most effective preventive measure.

---

## Avoid Secondhand Smoke

Reduce exposure whenever possible.

---

## Test for Radon

Especially in homes and workplaces.

---

## Use Workplace Safety Measures

Protection against occupational carcinogens.

---

## Maintain Healthy Lifestyle Habits

* Regular exercise
* Healthy diet
* Weight management

---

# Prognosis

Prognosis depends on:

* Stage at diagnosis
* Cancer type
* Genetic mutations
* Overall health
* Response to treatment

### Early-Stage Disease

Often potentially curable.

### Advanced Disease

Treatment focuses on prolonging survival and improving quality of life.

---

# Living with Lung Cancer

Supportive care may include:

### Pulmonary Rehabilitation

Exercises to improve breathing.

### Nutritional Support

Maintaining adequate nutrition during treatment.

### Psychological Counseling

Managing emotional stress.

### Palliative Care

Improving comfort and quality of life.

---

# Key Takeaways

* Lung cancer is one of the leading causes of cancer-related deaths worldwide.
* The two major types are **Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)** and **Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC)**.
* Smoking is the most significant risk factor.
* Common symptoms include persistent cough, chest pain, shortness of breath, and coughing up blood.
* Low-dose CT scans help detect cancer early in high-risk individuals.
* Diagnosis is confirmed through imaging and biopsy.
* Treatment options include surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, targeted therapy, and immunotherapy.
* Early detection significantly improves survival rates.
* Smoking prevention and cessation remain the most effective ways to reduce lung cancer risk.
