# 10. Cancer Risk Factors

## Overview

A **risk factor** is anything that increases the likelihood of developing cancer. Having one or more risk factors does not guarantee that a person will develop cancer, and some people develop cancer without any known risk factors.

Understanding cancer risk factors is important because many cancers can be prevented by reducing exposure to modifiable risks and adopting healthier lifestyles.

Cancer risk factors can be divided into:

1. Non-Modifiable Risk Factors
2. Modifiable Risk Factors
3. Environmental and Occupational Risk Factors
4. Infectious Causes of Cancer
5. Genetic and Hereditary Factors

---

# What Causes Cancer?

Cancer develops when mutations occur in the DNA of cells.

These mutations may:

* Activate cancer-promoting genes (oncogenes)
* Disable tumor suppressor genes
* Prevent damaged cells from dying

Over time, the accumulation of genetic changes can lead to uncontrolled cell growth and cancer.

---

# 1. Non-Modifiable Risk Factors

These are factors that cannot be changed.

---

## Age

### Overview

Cancer risk increases with age because genetic mutations accumulate over time.

### Why Age Matters

As people age:

* Cells undergo more divisions.
* DNA damage accumulates.
* Repair mechanisms become less effective.

### Examples

Many cancers are more common after age 50, including:

* Breast cancer
* Prostate cancer
* Colorectal cancer
* Lung cancer

---

## Gender

Certain cancers occur more frequently in one sex.

### Examples

Women:

* Breast cancer
* Cervical cancer
* Ovarian cancer

Men:

* Prostate cancer
* Testicular cancer

---

## Family History

Having close relatives with cancer may increase risk.

### High-Risk Relatives

* Parents
* Siblings
* Children

### Why It Matters

Family members may share:

* Genetic mutations
* Environmental exposures
* Lifestyle habits

---

## Genetic Mutations

Some inherited gene mutations significantly increase cancer risk.

### Examples

#### BRCA1 and BRCA2

Associated with:

* Breast cancer
* Ovarian cancer
* Prostate cancer

---

#### TP53

Associated with multiple cancer types.

---

#### APC

Associated with familial colorectal cancer syndromes.

---

# 2. Lifestyle-Related Risk Factors

Many cancer risk factors are linked to lifestyle and are potentially preventable.

---

## Tobacco Use

### Overview

Tobacco is the leading preventable cause of cancer worldwide.

### Harmful Products

* Cigarettes
* Cigars
* Pipes
* Smokeless tobacco

### Cancer Types Associated with Tobacco

* Lung cancer
* Oral cancer
* Throat cancer
* Esophageal cancer
* Bladder cancer
* Pancreatic cancer
* Kidney cancer

### Mechanism

Tobacco smoke contains thousands of chemicals, including many carcinogens that damage DNA.

---

## Secondhand Smoke

Non-smokers exposed to tobacco smoke also have increased cancer risk.

Especially linked to:

* Lung cancer
* Respiratory diseases

---

## Alcohol Consumption

### Overview

Alcohol increases the risk of several cancers.

### Cancer Types Associated

* Liver cancer
* Breast cancer
* Colorectal cancer
* Esophageal cancer
* Oral cancer

### Mechanism

Alcohol can:

* Damage DNA
* Increase inflammation
* Produce carcinogenic byproducts

---

## Poor Diet

### High-Risk Dietary Patterns

* Excess processed meat
* Excess red meat
* High-fat diets
* Low fruit and vegetable intake

### Effects

May contribute to:

* Colorectal cancer
* Stomach cancer
* Pancreatic cancer

---

## Obesity

### Overview

Obesity is linked to increased risk of multiple cancers.

### Associated Cancers

* Breast cancer
* Endometrial cancer
* Colorectal cancer
* Kidney cancer
* Liver cancer

### Mechanism

Obesity can cause:

* Chronic inflammation
* Hormonal imbalances
* Insulin resistance

---

## Physical Inactivity

Lack of exercise increases cancer risk.

### Benefits of Physical Activity

* Weight control
* Improved immune function
* Reduced inflammation

---

# 3. Environmental Risk Factors

---

## Ultraviolet (UV) Radiation

### Sources

* Sunlight
* Tanning beds

### Associated Cancer

* Skin cancer
* Melanoma

### Prevention

* Sunscreen
* Protective clothing
* Limiting sun exposure

---

## Air Pollution

Long-term exposure increases risk for:

* Lung cancer
* Respiratory diseases

---

## Radon Gas

### Overview

Naturally occurring radioactive gas.

### Risk

Second leading cause of lung cancer after smoking.

---

## Ionizing Radiation

Examples:

* X-rays
* Gamma rays
* Nuclear radiation

High exposure increases cancer risk.

---

# 4. Occupational Risk Factors

Certain occupations expose workers to carcinogens.

---

## Asbestos

Associated with:

* Lung cancer
* Mesothelioma

---

## Benzene

Associated with:

* Leukemia

---

## Arsenic

Associated with:

* Skin cancer
* Lung cancer

---

## Diesel Exhaust

Associated with:

* Lung cancer

---

## Industrial Chemicals

Some manufacturing chemicals increase cancer risk.

---

# 5. Infectious Causes of Cancer

Certain infections increase cancer risk.

---

## Human Papillomavirus (HPV)

### Associated Cancers

* Cervical cancer
* Anal cancer
* Penile cancer
* Oropharyngeal cancer

### Prevention

HPV vaccination.

---

## Hepatitis B Virus (HBV)

### Associated Cancer

* Liver cancer

### Prevention

HBV vaccination.

---

## Hepatitis C Virus (HCV)

### Associated Cancer

* Liver cancer

---

## Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV)

Associated with:

* Certain lymphomas
* Nasopharyngeal cancer

---

## Helicobacter pylori

A bacterial infection linked to:

* Stomach cancer

---

# 6. Hormonal Factors

Hormones influence the growth of certain cancers.

---

## Estrogen Exposure

Long-term exposure may increase risk of:

* Breast cancer
* Endometrial cancer

---

## Hormone Replacement Therapy

Extended use may slightly increase certain cancer risks.

---

# 7. Immune System Factors

A weakened immune system increases cancer susceptibility.

Examples include:

* Organ transplant recipients
* HIV infection
* Long-term immunosuppressive therapy

Common cancers:

* Lymphoma
* Kaposi Sarcoma

---

# 8. Chronic Inflammation

Persistent inflammation can promote cancer development.

Examples:

* Chronic hepatitis → Liver cancer
* Chronic gastritis → Stomach cancer
* Inflammatory bowel disease → Colorectal cancer

Related diseases include:

* Ulcerative Colitis
* Crohn's Disease

---

# Reducing Cancer Risk

Many cancers can be prevented by reducing exposure to known risk factors.

### Recommended Strategies

#### Avoid Tobacco

The most effective cancer prevention measure.

---

#### Limit Alcohol

Reduce consumption whenever possible.

---

#### Maintain Healthy Weight

Helps lower risk for multiple cancers.

---

#### Exercise Regularly

Aim for consistent physical activity.

---

#### Eat a Balanced Diet

Increase:

* Fruits
* Vegetables
* Whole grains

Limit:

* Processed foods
* Excess red meat

---

#### Protect Against UV Radiation

Use sunscreen and protective clothing.

---

#### Get Vaccinated

Important vaccines include:

* Gardasil 9
* Cervarix
* Engerix-B

---

#### Participate in Screening Programs

Examples:

* Mammography
* Colonoscopy
* Pap testing

---

# Key Takeaways

* Cancer develops due to genetic mutations influenced by multiple risk factors.
* Some risk factors are non-modifiable, such as age and genetics.
* Many risk factors are preventable, including smoking, alcohol use, obesity, poor diet, and physical inactivity.
* Environmental exposures such as UV radiation, radon, and occupational chemicals contribute to cancer risk.
* Certain infections, including HPV and hepatitis viruses, are important causes of cancer.
* Healthy lifestyle choices, vaccination, and regular screening can significantly reduce cancer risk.
* Understanding risk factors helps individuals make informed decisions to protect their health.
