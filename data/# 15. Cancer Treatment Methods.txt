# 15. Cancer Treatment Methods

## Overview

Cancer treatment involves various medical approaches designed to destroy cancer cells, control cancer growth, prevent recurrence, and improve quality of life. The choice of treatment depends on several factors, including:

* Cancer type
* Cancer stage
* Tumor location
* Patient age
* Overall health
* Genetic and molecular characteristics of the tumor

Many patients receive a combination of treatments, known as **multimodal therapy**, to achieve the best outcomes.

---

# Goals of Cancer Treatment

Treatment may have different objectives.

## Curative Treatment

Aim:

* Completely eliminate cancer.
* Achieve long-term remission.

Example:

* Early-stage breast cancer treated with surgery.

---

## Control Treatment

Aim:

* Slow cancer growth.
* Prevent progression.

Example:

* Advanced cancers managed with targeted therapy.

---

## Palliative Treatment

Aim:

* Relieve symptoms.
* Improve quality of life.

Examples:

* Pain control
* Symptom management
* Palliative radiation therapy

---

# Major Cancer Treatment Methods

The primary cancer treatments include:

1. Surgery

---

# 1. Surgery

## Overview

Surgery is one of the oldest and most effective cancer treatments.

### Purpose

* Remove tumors
* Obtain biopsy samples
* Prevent cancer spread
* Relieve symptoms

---

## Types of Cancer Surgery

### Diagnostic Surgery

Performed to obtain tissue samples.

Example:

* Biopsy procedures

---

### Curative Surgery

Removes all visible cancer.

Example:

* Removal of localized tumors

---

### Debulking Surgery

Removes as much tumor as possible when complete removal is difficult.

---

### Palliative Surgery

Relieves symptoms caused by tumors.

Examples:

* Obstruction relief
* Pain reduction

---

## Advantages

* Immediate tumor removal
* Potential cure for localized cancers

---

## Limitations

* Not effective for widespread metastatic disease
* Surgical risks and recovery time

---

# 2. Radiation Therapy

## Overview

Radiation therapy uses high-energy radiation to destroy cancer cells.

### Mechanism

Radiation damages DNA, preventing cancer cells from reproducing.

---

## Types of Radiation Therapy

### External Beam Radiation Therapy (EBRT)

Most common form.

Radiation is delivered from a machine outside the body.

---

### Internal Radiation Therapy (Brachytherapy)

Radioactive material is placed inside or near the tumor.

---

### Systemic Radiation Therapy

Radioactive substances travel through the bloodstream.

Example:

* Iodine-131

Used for thyroid cancer.

---

## Side Effects

* Fatigue
* Skin irritation
* Hair loss in treated areas
* Tissue inflammation

---

# 3. Chemotherapy

## Overview

Chemotherapy uses drugs to kill rapidly dividing cells.

### Purpose

* Destroy cancer cells
* Prevent spread
* Reduce recurrence risk

---

## Methods of Administration

### Intravenous (IV)

Most common method.

---

### Oral

Tablets or capsules.

---

### Injection

Direct administration into tissues.

---

## Common Chemotherapy Drugs

Examples:

* Cisplatin
* Carboplatin
* Doxorubicin
* Paclitaxel

---

## Side Effects

* Hair loss
* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Fatigue
* Increased infection risk

---

# 4. Immunotherapy

## Overview

Immunotherapy helps the immune system recognize and attack cancer cells.

### Importance

One of the most significant advances in modern oncology.

---

## Types of Immunotherapy

### Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors

Block proteins that prevent immune cells from attacking cancer.

Examples:

* Pembrolizumab
* Nivolumab
* Ipilimumab

---

### CAR-T Cell Therapy

Patient immune cells are genetically modified to attack cancer.

Examples:

Used in:

* Leukemia
* Lymphoma

---

### Cancer Vaccines

Examples:

* Sipuleucel-T

---

## Side Effects

* Fatigue
* Skin reactions
* Autoimmune complications

---

# 5. Targeted Therapy

## Overview

Targeted therapies attack specific molecular abnormalities in cancer cells.

### Advantages

* More precise than chemotherapy
* Often fewer side effects

---

## Examples

### EGFR Inhibitors

* Erlotinib
* Osimertinib

Used in some lung cancers.

---

### HER2-Targeted Therapy

* Trastuzumab

Used in HER2-positive breast cancer.

---

### BRAF Inhibitors

* Vemurafenib

Used in melanoma.

---

# 6. Hormone Therapy

## Overview

Certain cancers depend on hormones for growth.

Hormone therapy blocks hormone production or action.

---

## Common Hormone-Sensitive Cancers

### Breast Cancer

May depend on estrogen.

---

### Prostate Cancer

May depend on testosterone.

---

## Examples

### Breast Cancer

* Tamoxifen
* Anastrozole

---

### Prostate Cancer

* Leuprolide
* Goserelin

---

## Side Effects

* Hot flashes
* Weight gain
* Mood changes
* Bone loss

---

# 7. Stem Cell Transplantation

## Overview

Used primarily for blood cancers.

### Purpose

Replace damaged bone marrow with healthy stem cells.

---

## Types

### Autologous Transplant

Patient's own stem cells are used.

---

### Allogeneic Transplant

Stem cells obtained from a donor.

---

## Common Applications

* Leukemia
* Lymphoma
* Multiple Myeloma

---

# 8. Precision Medicine

## Overview

Precision medicine tailors treatment based on the unique characteristics of a patient's cancer.

---

## Molecular Testing

Identifies mutations such as:

* EGFR
* BRAF
* KRAS
* BRCA1
* BRCA2

---

## Benefits

* Personalized treatment
* Better treatment response
* Reduced unnecessary toxicity

---

# 9. Palliative Care

## Overview

Palliative care focuses on symptom management and quality of life.

### Applicable To

* Any cancer stage
* Not limited to end-of-life care

---

## Common Services

### Pain Management

Control of cancer-related pain.

---

### Nutritional Support

Addressing weight loss and weakness.

---

### Psychological Support

Managing anxiety and depression.

---

### Symptom Relief

Managing:

* Nausea
* Fatigue
* Breathlessness

---

# Combination Therapy

Examples:

### Breast Cancer

* Surgery
* Chemotherapy
* Radiation
* Hormone therapy

---

### Colorectal Cancer

* Surgery
* Chemotherapy
* Targeted therapy

---

### Lung Cancer

* Surgery
* Radiation

* Targeted therapy

Designed for individual tumors.

---

# Key Takeaways

* Cancer treatment aims to cure, control, or relieve symptoms of cancer.
* Major treatment methods include surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, targeted therapy, hormone therapy, stem cell transplantation, and palliative care.
* Modern cancer care increasingly uses precision medicine and molecular testing.
* Many patients benefit from combination therapy.
* Treatment selection depends on cancer type, stage, molecular characteristics, and patient health.
* Immunotherapy and targeted therapy have significantly improved outcomes for many cancers.
* Palliative care is important at all stages of cancer management.
* Emerging technologies such as AI, gene therapy, and personalized vaccines are shaping the future of cancer treatment.
