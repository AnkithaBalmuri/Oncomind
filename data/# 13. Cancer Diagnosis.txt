# 13. Cancer Diagnosis

## Overview

Cancer diagnosis is the process of determining whether a person has cancer, identifying the type of cancer, determining its location, and assessing how far it has spread. Accurate diagnosis is essential because treatment decisions depend on the specific characteristics of the cancer.

A cancer diagnosis typically involves multiple steps, including medical history, physical examination, laboratory tests, imaging studies, biopsy procedures, and molecular testing.

---

# What is Cancer Diagnosis?

Cancer diagnosis is the systematic evaluation of a patient to confirm the presence of cancer and gather information needed for treatment planning.

### Objectives

* Confirm whether cancer is present
* Identify the cancer type
* Determine the stage of disease
* Assess tumor characteristics
* Guide treatment decisions
* Predict prognosis

---

# Steps in Cancer Diagnosis

The diagnostic process generally follows these stages:

1. Medical History
2. Physical Examination
3. Laboratory Tests
4. Imaging Studies
5. Biopsy
6. Pathology Analysis
7. Molecular and Genetic Testing
8. Cancer Staging

---

# Medical History

## Purpose

To gather information about symptoms, risk factors, and overall health.

### Information Collected

#### Symptoms

Examples:

* Weight loss
* Fatigue
* Pain
* Bleeding
* Persistent cough

---

#### Family History

Important for hereditary cancers.

Examples:

* Breast cancer
* Ovarian cancer
* Colorectal cancer

---

#### Lifestyle Factors

Including:

* Smoking
* Alcohol use
* Diet
* Physical activity

---

#### Previous Medical Conditions

Examples:

* Chronic infections
* Inflammatory diseases
* Prior cancers

---

# Physical Examination

## Purpose

To identify visible or palpable abnormalities.

### Assessment May Include

#### Lumps or Masses

Examples:

* Breast lumps
* Neck masses

---

#### Enlarged Lymph Nodes

Common locations:

* Neck
* Armpits
* Groin

---

#### Skin Changes

Examples:

* Suspicious moles
* Non-healing sores

---

#### Organ Enlargement

Examples:

* Liver enlargement
* Spleen enlargement

---

# Laboratory Tests

Laboratory tests provide supportive information but usually cannot diagnose cancer alone.

---

## Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Evaluates:

* Red blood cells
* White blood cells
* Platelets

May help detect:

* Leukemia
* Lymphoma
* Bone marrow disorders

---

## Blood Chemistry Tests

Evaluate organ function.

### Examples

#### Liver Function Tests

May indicate liver involvement.

---

#### Kidney Function Tests

Assess kidney health.

---

#### Electrolyte Levels

Evaluate metabolic abnormalities.

---

# Tumor Markers

## What are Tumor Markers?

Substances produced by cancer cells or the body in response to cancer.

---

## Common Tumor Markers

| Marker  | Associated Cancer |
| ------- | ----------------- |
| PSA     | Prostate Cancer   |
| AFP     | Liver Cancer      |
| CEA     | Colorectal Cancer |
| CA-125  | Ovarian Cancer    |
| CA 19-9 | Pancreatic Cancer |

### Important Note

Tumor markers alone cannot confirm cancer.

---

# Imaging Studies

Imaging tests help locate tumors and assess spread.

---

## X-Ray

### Uses

* Lung abnormalities
* Bone lesions

### Advantages

* Quick
* Widely available

---

## Ultrasound

### Uses

* Breast masses
* Liver lesions
* Thyroid nodules

### Advantages

* No radiation
* Real-time imaging

---

## Computed Tomography (CT Scan)

### Overview

Uses X-rays to create detailed cross-sectional images.

### Uses

* Tumor detection
* Staging
* Treatment planning

---

## Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

### Overview

Uses magnetic fields and radio waves.

### Uses

* Brain tumors
* Spinal tumors
* Soft tissue cancers

---

## Positron Emission Tomography (PET Scan)

### Overview

Detects areas of increased metabolic activity.

### Uses

* Cancer detection
* Staging
* Monitoring treatment response

---

## PET-CT

Combines:

* PET imaging
* CT imaging

Provides both structural and functional information.

---

# Endoscopic Procedures

Endoscopy allows direct visualization of internal organs.

---

## Colonoscopy

Examines:

* Colon
* Rectum

Used to detect:

* Polyps
* Colorectal cancer

---

## Bronchoscopy

Examines airways and lungs.

Used for:

* Lung cancer diagnosis

---

## Upper Gastrointestinal Endoscopy

Examines:

* Esophagus
* Stomach
* Duodenum

---

## Cystoscopy

Examines the bladder.

---

# Biopsy

## What is a Biopsy?

A biopsy involves removing tissue for microscopic examination.

### Importance

A biopsy is the gold standard for cancer diagnosis.

---

# Types of Biopsy

## Fine Needle Aspiration (FNA)

Uses a thin needle to collect cells.

### Advantages

* Minimally invasive
* Quick

---

## Core Needle Biopsy

Removes a small cylinder of tissue.

Provides more information than FNA.

---

## Incisional Biopsy

Removes part of a suspicious lesion.

---

## Excisional Biopsy

Removes the entire lesion.

---

## Surgical Biopsy

Performed when other methods are insufficient.

---

# Pathology Examination

## Role of the Pathologist

A pathologist examines tissue samples under a microscope.

### Determines

* Presence of cancer
* Cancer type
* Tumor grade
* Margins
* Biomarker status

---

# Histopathology

## What is Histopathology?

Microscopic study of diseased tissues.

### Information Obtained

* Cell appearance
* Growth patterns
* Aggressiveness

---

# Cancer Grading

Grade describes how abnormal cancer cells appear.

---

## Grade 1 (Low Grade)

* Well differentiated
* Slow-growing

---

## Grade 2

* Moderately differentiated

---

## Grade 3

* Poorly differentiated
* More aggressive

---

## Grade 4

* Highly abnormal
* Fast-growing

---

# Immunohistochemistry (IHC)

## Purpose

Uses antibodies to identify proteins on cancer cells.

### Applications

* Classifying cancer types
* Identifying treatment targets

---

## Examples

### Breast Cancer

Tests:

* ER (Estrogen Receptor)
* PR (Progesterone Receptor)
* HER2

---

### Lymphoma

Helps identify lymphoma subtypes.

---

# Molecular and Genetic Testing

Modern cancer diagnosis increasingly relies on molecular analysis.

---

## Genetic Mutations

Examples:

### BRCA1 and BRCA2

Associated with:

* Breast cancer
* Ovarian cancer

---

### EGFR

Associated with lung cancer.

---

### KRAS

Associated with colorectal cancer.

---

### BRAF

Associated with melanoma.

---

# Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)

## What is NGS?

Advanced technology that analyzes multiple genes simultaneously.

### Benefits

* Identifies actionable mutations
* Supports personalized treatment

---

# Liquid Biopsy

## Definition

A blood test that detects tumor-derived DNA.

### Advantages

* Minimally invasive
* Can monitor treatment response
* Useful for detecting recurrence

---

# Cancer Staging

Staging determines how far cancer has spread.

---

## TNM Staging System

### T (Tumor)

Size and local extent of the tumor.

---

### N (Nodes)

Involvement of lymph nodes.

---

### M (Metastasis)

Presence of distant spread.

---

# Stage Grouping

## Stage 0

Cancer in situ.

---

## Stage I

Localized cancer.

---

## Stage II

Larger tumor or limited spread.

---

## Stage III

Regional spread.

---

## Stage IV

Metastatic disease.

---

# Challenges in Cancer Diagnosis

## False Positives

Abnormal results despite absence of cancer.

---

## False Negatives

Cancer present despite normal test results.

---

## Tumor Heterogeneity

Different parts of a tumor may have different characteristics.

---

## Rare Cancers

May require specialized testing.

---

## Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Applications include:

* Radiology

* Risk prediction

---

## Multi-Cancer Early Detection Tests

Blood tests designed to detect multiple cancers simultaneously.

---

## Advanced Imaging

Improved accuracy in identifying small tumors.

---

# Importance of Early Diagnosis

Benefits include:

* Higher cure rates
* More treatment options
* Reduced complications
* Improved survival
* Better quality of life

---

# Key Takeaways

* Cancer diagnosis involves a combination of clinical evaluation, laboratory tests, imaging studies, biopsy, pathology, and molecular testing.
* A biopsy remains the gold standard for confirming cancer.
* Imaging techniques such as CT, MRI, PET, and ultrasound help locate and stage tumors.
* Pathology and molecular testing provide critical information about cancer type and behavior.
* Tumor markers can support diagnosis and monitoring but cannot diagnose cancer alone.
* Cancer staging helps guide treatment decisions and predict prognosis.
* Emerging technologies such as liquid biopsy, next-generation sequencing, and AI are transforming cancer diagnosis.
* Early and accurate diagnosis is essential for effective treatment and improved outcomes.
